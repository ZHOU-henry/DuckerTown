# Reference Patterns

These are reference patterns, not templates to copy.

## What Duckertown should learn from

### `DuckerChat`

Useful lesson:

- visible multi-agent social structure matters
- humans need to inspect relationships, dissent, and belief shifts

What Duckertown borrows:

- relationship visibility
- agent identity boundaries
- claim and memory discipline

What Duckertown adds:

- space
- bodies
- routine
- town economics
- place-based interaction

### `AI Town`

Useful lesson:

- a small town is an ideal object for generative agent experimentation
- the right early world is not a huge open-world map but a compact social loop

What Duckertown borrows:

- town-first simulation scope
- agent memories and town interaction framing
- local interactive observation

What Duckertown rejects:

- a narrow demo-town that cannot evolve into a more general world system

Reference:

- `https://github.com/a16z-infra/ai-town`

### `Smallville / generative agents` line

Useful lesson:

- long-term memory, reflection, and social planning can produce believable
  social life in small communities

What Duckertown borrows:

- memory-reflection-action loop
- social routine framing
- event-triggered behavior shifts

What Duckertown rejects:

- treating the project mainly as a paper demo rather than as a playable and
  extensible world system

Reference:

- `https://github.com/joonspk-research/generative_agents`

### `Neighborly`

Useful lesson:

- procedural social simulation benefits from strong data models for
  relationships, settlements, and events

What Duckertown borrows:

- simulation-state discipline
- relationship-driven world evolution
- town-scale procedural generation logic

Reference:

- `https://github.com/ShiJbey/neighborly`

### `One Hour One Life` and persistent social worlds

Useful lesson:

- society becomes much more legible when place, scarcity, labor, and lineage
  matter over time

What Duckertown borrows:

- persistent world instinct
- social consequences tied to time and place
- emergent civilization framing

What Duckertown rejects:

- building around one ultra-specific survival loop before agent society works

### `Stardew Valley` style readability

Useful lesson:

- small-scale pixel worlds can feel emotionally legible and socially dense

What Duckertown borrows:

- 2D top-down town readability
- cozy social density
- everyday-life rhythm

What Duckertown rejects:

- copying proprietary game content, art, or assets

## Asset rule

Duckertown should not use proprietary Stardew Valley art or GIFs on the public
site or in the repo.

Preferred public asset sources:

- permissive or CC0 pixel asset packs
- self-made mockups
- clearly licensed open screenshots or tilesets
