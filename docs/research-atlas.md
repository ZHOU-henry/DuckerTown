# Research Atlas

## Goal

Learn broadly from existing projects related to:

- agent towns
- AI NPCs
- generative worlds
- social simulation
- persistent life-sim systems

without copying their code or product surface.

## Project clusters

### `AI Town`

Why it matters:

- compact town scale
- many-agent local interaction
- directly relevant to early Duckertown framing

Reference:

- `https://github.com/a16z-infra/ai-town`

### `generative agents / Smallville`

Why it matters:

- memory, reflection, social planning
- believable social life from agent internals

Reference:

- `https://github.com/joonspk-research/generative_agents`

### `Neighborly`

Why it matters:

- procedural settlement and social simulation logic
- stronger simulation-state modeling

Reference:

- `https://github.com/ShiJbey/neighborly`

### `Persistent social world games`

Why they matter:

- place and scarcity affect society
- routines and labor create structure
- social memory becomes legible through world continuity

Duckertown should study them for:

- world pressure
- long-term interaction consequence
- readability of town-scale simulation

## Synthesis

Duckertown should combine:

- the compact experimental town instinct of AI Town
- the memory-reflection-action loop of generative agents
- the stronger simulation-state discipline of Neighborly
- the lived-in readability of cozy pixel life-sim games

Its distinctive move should be:

- to turn agent society into a spatial, inspectable, playable world
